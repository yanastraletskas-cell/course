Mini-Course: Secure Your Facility in 5 Days

A 5-lesson email course for Engineering Managers and Facilities Directors who want to modernize access control and time tracking — without a long IT project.

01
Day 1 · Foundation

Why Manual Access Control Is Costing You More Than You Think

The hidden costs most managers never calculate
▾

The Problem

Most engineering companies track access with key cards, sign-in sheets, or basic fob systems installed 10+ years ago. They feel "good enough." They're not.

What You're Actually Losing

  • Time theft: 8–12 minutes/day per employee in clock-in errors, buddy punching, and early departures. At $38/hr with 75 employees, that's $42,000/year.
  • Incident liability: Without access logs, you can't prove who was in a restricted zone during an incident. That's a legal and insurance problem.
  • Admin overhead: Manual sign-in reconciliation for payroll and compliance audits consumes 8–15 hours/month for most facilities managers.
  • Compliance gaps: ISO 27001, OSHA, and building codes increasingly require documented access trails. Manual logs don't meet the standard.
Today's action: Estimate your current time-theft cost. Take your employee count × average wage × 8 minutes × 250 working days. Most managers are shocked. Use our ROI Calculator to get a precise number.
02
Day 2 · Mapping

How to Audit Your Current Access Zones (in 1 Hour)

A simple framework for identifying gaps before you spend anything
▾

The Zone Audit Framework

Before buying any system, you need to know what you're protecting. Walk your facility and categorize every entry point into 3 tiers:

Tier Classification

  • Tier 1 — Open: Reception, common areas, parking. Low security, high traffic. Basic logging needed.
  • Tier 2 — Controlled: Offices, meeting rooms, stockrooms. Role-based access required. Time logging essential.
  • Tier 3 — Restricted: Server rooms, labs, data centers, hazardous material storage. Granular permissions + instant alert on unauthorized attempt.

Questions to Answer for Each Door

  • Who should have access? (Role or individual)
  • During what hours? (Business hours only? 24/7?)
  • Do you need an alert if access is denied?
  • Does this door feed into payroll? (Clock-in/out point)
Today's action: Do a physical walk of your facility. Count your Tier 2 and Tier 3 doors. Most companies have 6–12 doors that need real access control. Note the ones where you have zero visibility today.
03
Day 3 · Integration

Connecting Door Access to Payroll: The Setup Most Companies Skip

How unified systems eliminate your payroll headaches
▾

The Silo Problem

Most companies run access control and time tracking in completely separate systems. The result: someone has to manually reconcile two data sources every week. This is where errors happen, and where hours of admin time disappear.

How a Unified System Works

  • Door entry = automatic clock-in. Door exit = automatic clock-out.
  • No manual punching. No buddy punching. No errors.
  • Hours data feeds directly to payroll integrations (ADP, Gusto, Paychex, BambooHR)
  • Overtime flags, late arrivals, and early departures are automatic

What to Ask Any Vendor

  • Does access event data automatically create time records?
  • Which payroll systems do you natively integrate with?
  • Can I set different clock-in rules per door or per employee group?
  • How do you handle mobile workers or off-site employees?
Today's action: Identify which payroll software your company currently uses. Confirm whether your current access system has a native integration. If not — that gap is costing you hours every month.
04
Day 4 · Security

Building an Audit-Ready Access Policy (Template Included)

What ISO, OSHA, and building codes actually require from you
▾

What Auditors Want to See

  • A documented access control policy (who approved what access, and when)
  • Complete logs of entry and exit events, retained for 12+ months
  • Evidence of access review (quarterly or annual review of who has access to what)
  • Incident records for denied access attempts
  • Offboarding procedures (how quickly is a terminated employee's access revoked?)

The 5-Minute Offboarding Test

The single best test of your access system: how long does it take to revoke all access for a terminated employee? If the answer is more than 5 minutes, you have a security gap. With a modern system, it's 2 clicks.

Access Policy Template (Core Elements)

  • Access tiers and who owns each tier
  • Approval workflow for granting new access
  • Automatic revocation triggers (termination, role change, contractor end date)
  • Quarterly access review schedule
  • Incident response protocol for denied access events
Today's action: Pull your last terminated employee's record. How long did it take to fully revoke their access? If you don't have a clear answer — that's what we'd fix on day one of your TimeClock 365 setup.
05
Day 5 · Implementation

Your 30-Day Rollout Plan: From Decision to Go-Live

A realistic timeline with zero disruption to your team
▾

Week 1: Decision & Scoping

  • Confirm door count and zones (use your Day 2 audit)
  • Confirm payroll integration requirements
  • Get IT sign-off on network requirements
  • Sign agreement and schedule installation

Week 2: Installation

  • Hardware install (typically 1 day for up to 10 doors)
  • Network configuration and cloud connection
  • Admin account setup and permission structure

Week 3: Employee Onboarding

  • Import employee roster from HR system
  • Assign access permissions by role
  • Issue new credentials (badge, PIN, or mobile app)
  • Test all doors and integrations

Week 4: Go Live

  • Cut over from old system
  • Run first payroll export from live time data
  • Brief your team (5-min walkthrough is all they need)
  • Review first week of access logs with your manager
